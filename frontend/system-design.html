1. Introduction

1.1 Background

Malawi's healthcare system operates under significant resource constraints, with paper-based record keeping remaining the norm in many facilities. The Malawi University of Science and Technology (MUST) Teaching Hospital, as a newly established tertiary healthcare institution, has identified the need for a modern electronic health record (EHR) and hospital management system to support its clinical, administrative, and operational workflows. Existing health information systems in Malawi are often fragmented, licensed at prohibitive cost, or designed without consideration for local infrastructure challenges such as intermittent power supply and unreliable internet connectivity.

1.2 Purpose

This document presents the system design specification for MUST Health Tech (MHT), a proposed web-based EHR and hospital management platform. The purpose of this document is to define the architecture, functional components, technology choices, and implementation plan for a prototype system that will demonstrate the feasibility of an integrated, offline-capable health information system tailored to the Malawian teaching hospital context. The prototype is to be developed as a proof-of-concept for academic evaluation and stakeholder review.

1.3 Scope

The proposed prototype shall address the following functional areas:

  • Clinical Workflow: Patient registration, triage and emergency assessment, appointment scheduling and queue management, clinical documentation using the SOAP (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan) format, vital signs recording with automatic BMI calculation, laboratory order placement and result entry, pharmacy prescribing and dispensing, inpatient ward management, critical care documentation (ICU), and dialysis session tracking.
  • Administrative Functions: Role-based user management, billing and payment processing with mobile money and bank transfer support, inventory and stock management, equipment maintenance tracking, incident reporting, audit logging, and quality indicator monitoring.
  • Patient Self-Service Portal: Appointment booking, bill payment and receipt generation, laboratory result viewing, prescription history access, and patient rights education in English and Chichewa.
  • Operational Dashboard: Aggregated views of hospital operations, disease surveillance monitoring, bed management status, and key performance indicator tracking with real-time WebSocket updates.
  • Offline Capability: Progressive Web App (PWA) deployment with service worker caching, IndexedDB local data storage, and background synchronisation queue for low-connectivity environments.

1.4 Target Users

The system shall serve the following user categories:

  • Healthcare professionals: Doctors, nurses, clinical officers, midwives, pharmacists, laboratory technicians, and radiographers who require efficient tools for clinical documentation and order management.
  • Hospital administrators: Department heads, hospital administrators, and data entry staff who need oversight of operations, reporting, and patient registration.
  • Patients: Individuals receiving care at MUST Teaching Hospital who shall have access to a self-service portal for appointment management, bill payment, and health information.
  • System administrators: Technical staff responsible for user management, system configuration, and maintenance.

1.5 Document Structure

This document is organised into twelve sections. Section 2 provides a system overview including goals and module descriptions. Sections 3 through 7 detail the technical architecture, technology stack, database design, API specification, and frontend design. Sections 8 and 9 cover security and offline/PWA considerations. Section 10 describes the user interface design. Section 11 addresses deployment. Section 12 concludes with evaluation criteria and future work.

2. System Overview

2.1 System Goals

The proposed MHT prototype shall achieve the following objectives:

  1. Digitise patient records — Replace paper-based charts with structured electronic records accessible from any authorised workstation within the hospital network, reducing lost records and improving data completeness.
  2. Streamline clinical workflows — Provide intuitive, task-oriented interfaces for registration, triage, consultation, prescribing, and lab ordering to reduce documentation time and minimise transcription errors.
  3. Enable data-driven management — Deliver aggregated dashboards and analytics to hospital administrators for monitoring operations, tracking disease patterns, and supporting resource allocation decisions.
  4. Empower patients — Offer patients direct digital access to their health information, appointment scheduling, and bill payment through a dedicated portal accessible from personal mobile devices.
  5. Operate reliably in low-connectivity settings — Ensure core functionality remains available during internet outages through local caching and queued synchronisation, reflecting the reality of network infrastructure at many Malawian health facilities.
  6. Provide a foundation for scaling — Design the architecture to support future expansion to multiple facilities, integration with national health information systems, and addition of advanced features such as clinical decision support.

3. System Architecture

3.1 Proposed Architectural Pattern

The MHT system shall follow a single-page application (SPA) communicating with a RESTful API backend. This architecture provides clear separation between the presentation layer and the business logic layer, enabling independent development, testing, and scaling of each component. The system shall use a three-tier model:

  1. Client Tier: A browser-based SPA implemented in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, served as a single HTML file. All user interface rendering and client-side logic executes in the browser. A service worker intercepts network requests for offline operation.
  2. Application Tier: A Python-based REST API server handling authentication, business logic, data validation, and persistence. The server also exposes a WebSocket endpoint for real-time dashboard updates.
  3. Data Tier: A relational database accessed through an Object-Relational Mapper (ORM). The prototype shall use SQLite for development and demonstration, with PostgreSQL as the production target.

3.3 Design Principles

The proposed architecture shall adhere to the following principles:

  • Separation of concerns: The frontend handles all presentation and user interaction logic. The backend manages business rules, data validation, authentication, and persistence. The API contract is defined by OpenAPI specification.
  • Stateless API: The backend shall not maintain client session state. All authentication information shall be carried in JWT tokens within HTTP headers, enabling horizontal scaling through simple load balancing.
  • Async-first backend: All database operations and HTTP calls shall use Python async/await to maximise concurrency and throughput under load.
  • Offline resilience: The client shall cache API responses locally and queue mutations for synchronisation when connectivity is restored, ensuring uninterrupted operation during network interruptions.
  • Configuration-driven deployment: Environment-specific settings (database URLs, secret keys, API credentials) shall be externalised via environment variables and loaded through a Pydantic Settings model, allowing deployment across development, staging, and production environments without code changes.

3.4 Component Interaction Flow

The SPA shall communicate with the backend exclusively through HTTP REST requests with JSON payloads. A single WebSocket connection shall provide real-time dashboard updates with a 10-second broadcast interval. The service worker shall operate as a transparent proxy: when online, it forwards requests to the network and caches responses; when offline, it serves cached responses and returns a designated offline signal for uncached resources. The frontend JavaScript shall respond to the offline signal by reading from IndexedDB for read operations and queuing write operations for later synchronisation.

3.5 Patient Portal Separation

The patient portal shall be implemented as a logically separate set of API endpoints under the /api/v1/patient/ prefix, with its own JWT authentication domain using a distinct secret key. For the prototype phase, patient data shall be stored in an in-memory data store seeded with demo records. This separation ensures that patient-facing functionality can be independently scaled, secured, and evolved without impacting clinical operations. In a production implementation, the patient portal backend would be migrated to use the same persistent database as the clinical system, with appropriate access controls.

4.2 Rationale for Key Decisions

Python for Backend Development

Python is widely taught at MUST and in Malawian ICT programmes, ensuring that the system can be maintained and extended by local developers. The FastAPI framework's async support provides performance competitive with Node.js while retaining Python's readability and extensive library ecosystem. The healthcare domain has strong Python support through libraries such as FHIR (fhir.resources), HL7 (python-hl7), and medical coding (icd10-cm).

Vanilla JavaScript Frontend

A framework-free approach is chosen for the prototype to eliminate build toolchain complexity (Webpack, Vite, Babel, etc.) and allow the entire frontend to be served as a single HTML file directly from the backend without a separate static asset pipeline. This simplifies initial development and evaluation. If the prototype proceeds to production, a component-based framework (React or Vue) could be adopted for improved maintainability.

SQLite for Prototype, PostgreSQL for Production

SQLite eliminates the need for database server setup during development and evaluation, allowing the prototype to run on any system with Python installed. SQLAlchemy's database abstraction layer ensures that all queries are written in a dialect-independent manner, making the transition to PostgreSQL a simple configuration change (OFFLINE_MODE=False + POSTGRES_URL setting) without code modifications.

PWA over Native Mobile App

A Progressive Web App is proposed for the patient portal and clinical mobile access because it eliminates app store distribution requirements, reduces development cost, and provides automatic updates. The PWA can be installed on any modern smartphone and behaves like a native app with full-screen display and offline support through the service worker.

5. Database Design

5.1 Design Approach

The database shall follow a modular schema design with domain-specific tables organised around the central patients table. The schema shall include approximately 35 tables covering all clinical and administrative domains. Every table shall include created_at and updated_at timestamp columns for audit purposes. Foreign key constraints shall enforce referential integrity at the database level.

5.2 Core Clinical Schema

patients

Central demographic record for every individual receiving care. Fields shall include:

  • id (Integer, Primary Key), passport_id (String, Unique) — the MHT-format identifier
  • surname, first_name, middle_name — patient name components
  • date_of_birth, gender (Enum: MALE/FEMALE/OTHER), blood_group
  • phone, email — contact information
  • home_district, traditional_authority, village — geographic location for public health reporting
  • emergency_contact_name, emergency_contact_relation, emergency_contact_phone
  • next_of_kin_name, next_of_kin_phone
  • occupation, marital_status
  • is_active, is_deceased, death_date, death_cause

visits

Records every patient encounter with the health system. Visit types shall include OPD, IPD, Emergency, Referral, Review, and Telemedicine. Each visit shall track triage category, presenting complaint, consultation status, admission/discharge dates, ward and bed assignment, and discharge disposition.

vital_signs

Physiological measurements linked to a visit. Fields shall include systolic and diastolic blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory rate, temperature, oxygen saturation, weight (kg), height (cm), BMI (auto-calculated), blood glucose, pain score (0-10), Glasgow Coma Scale (eye/verbal/motor components and total), and MUAC (Mid-Upper Arm Circumference) for nutritional assessment.

clinical_notes

Clinical documentation in SOAP (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan) format. Notes shall support multiple types (consultation, progress, discharge summary, procedure note) and include a signing workflow with optional cosigning.

5.3 Appointments and Queue Schema

appointments

Scheduled patient appointments with support for status tracking, priority assignment, and teleconsultation. Status values shall include: SCHEDULED, CONFIRMED, IN_PROGRESS, COMPLETED, CANCELLED, DID_NOT_ATTEND, RESCHEDULED. Priority levels: ROUTINE, URGENT, EMERGENCY, FOLLOW_UP. The table shall also track reminder delivery status and cancellation reasons.

queue

Real-time patient queue positions within departments. Each entry shall track queue position, department, priority level, status (waiting/called/in consultation/completed), room assignment, and target clinician.

5.4 Diagnostics Schema

lab_orders + lab_tests

A two-table design where lab_orders represents the overall order (with auto-generated order number, clinician, specimen tracking, and status workflow) and lab_tests represents individual test requests within each order (test name, result value, reference range, abnormal flag). Radiology orders shall be stored in a separate radiology_orders table.

5.5 Pharmacy Schema

prescriptions + prescription_items

A two-table design for medication prescribing. The prescriptions table tracks the overall order (prescriber, status, clinical indication). The prescription_items table stores individual medications with dosage, route, frequency, duration, and dispensing status. A separate medication_administrations table shall record each administered dose for inpatients.

drug_stock

Pharmacy inventory tracking with batch numbers, expiry dates, reorder levels, controlled substance flags, and supplier information.

5.6 Billing Schema

bills

Patient financial records with title, description, amount, status (unpaid/paid/cancelled), payment method, account number, unique receipt reference, and payment timestamp.

5.7 Administration Schema

users

Staff accounts with username, email, bcrypt-hashed password, role (16-level enum), professional designation, licence number, department, facility assignment, login tracking (failed attempts, lockout timestamp), and WhatsApp notification opt-in status.

audit_trails / audit_logs

Two tables for tracking user actions and system-level changes. Each entry shall record the acting user, action type, affected resource, timestamp, IP address, and detailed changes where applicable.

5.8 Additional Schemas

The complete schema shall also include tables for inventory (items, stock movements, equipment maintenance), critical care (ICU admissions, severity scores, fluid balance), dialysis (sessions, CKD records), governance (incident reports, quality indicators, mortality reviews), announcements, notifications (log), departments, and facilities.

5.9 Database Configuration

The SQLite database for the prototype shall be configured with Write-Ahead Logging (WAL) mode to allow concurrent read operations during write transactions. Foreign key constraints shall be explicitly enabled via PRAGMA foreign_keys = ON. The database file shall reside at backend/ehealth_malawi.db.

The backend shall expose a RESTful JSON API under the /api/v1/ URL prefix. All endpoints shall follow consistent naming conventions: plural nouns for resource collections (/patients, /appointments), HTTP methods for CRUD operations (GET for retrieval, POST for creation, PUT for update, DELETE for removal), and nested routes for sub-resources (/patients/{id}/visits). The API shall be self-documenting via automatically generated OpenAPI (Swagger) documentation available at the /docs endpoint.

6.3 Authentication for API Access

All endpoints except the following shall require a valid JWT bearer token in the Authorization: Bearer <token> header: POST /auth/login, POST /auth/register, POST /patient/register, POST /patient/login, and GET /info/announcements (public). The main system shall use an HS256-signed JWT with an 8-hour expiry period. The patient portal shall use a separate signing key to maintain authentication domain isolation.

6.4 Real-Time Dashboard

A WebSocket endpoint at /api/v1/ws/dashboard shall provide real-time updates. When a client connects (authenticated via a token query parameter), the server shall register the connection with a ConnectionManager and begin broadcasting aggregated operational data every 10 seconds. The broadcast shall include bed occupancy, queue lengths, stock alerts, and disease surveillance counts. Disconnected clients shall be automatically cleaned up.

6.5 Response Format

All responses shall use a consistent JSON structure. Successful GET requests shall return the requested resource or array directly. Successful POST requests shall return the created resource with HTTP 201 status. Errors shall return an appropriate HTTP status code with a JSON body containing a detail field with a human-readable error message. Validation errors shall return 400, authentication failures 401, permission errors 403, and not-found errors 404.

7. Frontend Design

7.1 Proposed Frontend Architecture

The frontend shall be implemented as a single-page application (SPA) contained within a single HTML file. This approach eliminates the need for a build toolchain and allows the frontend to be served directly by the backend without a separate static file server. All CSS and JavaScript shall be embedded within the HTML file. The SPA shall manage page transitions by toggling the display CSS property of page <div> elements based on user navigation.

7.2 Multi-Portal Navigation Model

The system shall implement two distinct navigation models for the two user populations:

Administrative Interface (Health Professional)

  • Navigation mechanism: A fixed left sidebar listing all available modules with icons and text labels. The active page shall be indicated with a coloured left border accent. The sidebar shall be collapsible via a hamburger menu toggle for narrow screens.
  • Header bar: Global search field, notification bell with badge, user profile dropdown, PWA install button, and an online/offline connection status indicator.
  • Content area: The remaining screen area shall display the selected page's content, which may include data tables, KPI cards, charts, and action buttons. Modal overlays shall handle data entry without navigating away from the current page.
  • Proposed pages (18): Dashboard (Home), Patient Registry, Appointments, Queue Management, Triage & Emergency, Clinical Notes, Vital Signs, Inpatient Ward, Critical Care (ICU), Dialysis & CKD, Laboratory, Pharmacy, Inventory, Billing & Payments, Governance, Administration, plus modal-based workflows.

Patient Portal

  • Navigation mechanism: A fixed bottom tab bar with five icons and labels. The active tab shall be highlighted with a purple accent colour (#a78bfa) to visually distinguish the patient experience from the admin interface.
  • Proposed tabs: Home (dashboard with greeting, KPI cards, allergies, conditions, upcoming appointments), Appts (appointment list, booking form, detail modal), Bills (bill list with pay flow including method selection, PIN entry, and receipt generation), Results (laboratory results display), More (tabbed panel with My Rights, Rate HCW, Suggestion Box, Corruption Report).
  • Profile: Accessible via a hamburger menu icon, displayed as a card-based layout with avatar upload support.

7.3 Proposed Client-Side Data Management

The frontend shall implement a three-tier data management strategy:

  1. Session state: JWT tokens and user preferences shall be stored in localStorage. The token shall be attached to all API requests via a wrapper function around the native fetch API.
  2. API response cache: Successful GET responses for key resources (patients, appointments, lab orders, prescriptions, bills) shall be stored in IndexedDB object stores for offline read access. The cache shall be updated on each successful fetch and used as fallback during network failures.
  3. Mutation queue: POST, PUT, and DELETE operations performed while offline shall be stored in a sync_queue IndexedDB object store. Each queue entry shall contain the HTTP method, endpoint URL, request body, timestamp, and retry count.

8. Authentication & Security

8.1 Proposed Authentication Architecture

The system shall implement a dual authentication domain model to separate clinical staff access from patient self-service access, each with appropriate security policies.

Health Professional Authentication Domain

  • Users shall authenticate via POST /api/v1/auth/login with username and password credentials.
  • Passwords shall be hashed using bcrypt (12 salt rounds) via the passlib library before storage in the database.
  • On successful authentication, the server shall issue a JWT token signed with the HS256 algorithm using a configurable secret key. The token shall encode the user ID, role, and an 8-hour expiration timestamp.
  • The client shall store the token in localStorage and attach it to all subsequent requests via the Authorization: Bearer <token> header.
  • The server shall validate the token on every protected request via a get_current_user dependency that decodes the token, fetches the user record, and verifies the account is active and not locked.
  • Role-based access shall be enforced through a require_role(roles) dependency that checks the authenticated user's role against a permitted list for each endpoint.

Patient Portal Authentication Domain

  • Patients shall authenticate using their passport ID (MHT-format identifier) and phone number — credentials that patients can reliably recall without needing to remember a username and password.
  • A separate JWT secret key (patient-secret-key) shall be used to sign patient tokens, ensuring that patient tokens cannot authenticate against clinical endpoints and vice versa.
  • The patient JWT shall encode the patient's passport ID, which is used to filter all data access to only the authenticated patient's records.
  • New patients shall register via POST /api/v1/patient/register providing personal details. The system shall auto-generate a unique passport ID in the MHT-XXXXXX format.

8.3 Data Privacy Considerations

  • Patient consent for data processing shall be obtained at registration (implied by use of the system).
  • The corruption reporting feature shall default to anonymous submission, concealing the reporter's identity unless explicitly opted out.
  • Account deletion shall be offered via the privacy settings, clearing personal data as appropriate.
  • Audit logs shall capture all access to patient records for accountability and breach investigation.
  • HTTPS shall be enforced in production deployment through a reverse proxy with TLS termination.

9. Offline & PWA Support

9.1 Requirement for Offline Operation

Internet connectivity in many Malawian healthcare facilities is characterised by frequent interruptions, low bandwidth, and high latency. The prototype shall address this constraint through an offline-first design that ensures core functionality remains available during network outages. The system shall synchronise data automatically when connectivity is restored.

9.2 Proposed Service Worker Strategy

A service worker registered at the root path shall implement the following caching strategies:

  • Cache-first for static assets: The application shell (index.html, manifest.json, PWA icons, Google Fonts, html2canvas, jsPDF) shall be pre-cached during service worker installation. On subsequent loads, these assets shall be served from cache with background updates when the network is available.
  • Network-first for API calls: All requests to /api/ shall attempt the network first. On success, the response shall be cached and returned to the application. On network failure, the cached response shall be served. If no cached response exists, a JSON response with {offline: true} shall be returned to trigger IndexedDB fallback in the application layer.

9.3 IndexedDB Client-Side Database

The frontend shall create and manage an IndexedDB database named ehealth_mw_offline with the following object stores:

Store NameKey PathPurpose
patientsidCached patient records for offline lookup
appointmentsidCached appointment data
lab_ordersidCached lab orders and results
prescriptionsidCached prescription records
billsidCached billing records
sync_queueAuto-incrementQueue of mutations performed while offline
metakeySync metadata, timestamps, and version tracking

9.4 Proposed Background Synchronisation Process

When the application detects a transition from offline to online state (via the window.online event), the following synchronisation process shall be triggered:

  1. Read all entries from the sync_queue store in FIFO order, filtering to those with status pending and retry count below 5.
  2. For each entry, replay the original HTTP request (method, URL, headers, body) against the live API.
  3. On successful response (2xx status code), mark the entry as synced and update the corresponding data cache with the server response.
  4. On network or server error, increment the retry count. Entries exceeding 5 retries shall be flagged as failed for manual review.
  5. Display a synchronisation status indicator in the application header showing the queue count and current sync status (idle/syncing/synced/failed).

9.5 Progressive Web App Features

  • Web App Manifest: A manifest.json file shall declare the application name, icons (192x192 and 512x512), theme colour (#1e293b), background colour (#0f172a), and display mode (standalone for full-screen experience).
  • Install Prompt: The application shall listen for the beforeinstallprompt event and display an install button. The button shall be hidden after the appinstalled event fires or if the app is already installed.
  • Offline Launch: When launched without network, the service worker shall serve the cached application shell, enabling the user to view previously loaded data and queue new entries for later synchronisation.

11.2 Proposed Startup Procedure

The prototype shall be started with the following commands:

# 1. Navigate to the backend directory
cd backend

# 2. Create and activate a virtual environment (first time only)
python -m venv venv
.\venv\Scripts\Activate   # Windows
# source venv/bin/activate  # macOS/Linux

# 3. Install dependencies (first time only)
pip install -r requirements.txt

# 4. Start the development server
python -m uvicorn app.main:app --host 127.0.0.1 --port 8000 --reload

The frontend shall be served directly by the backend at http://127.0.0.1:8000. No separate web server or build step is required. The API documentation shall be available at http://127.0.0.1:8000/docs.

11.3 Docker-Based Deployment

For consistent deployment across different environments, a Docker setup is proposed: 

# Using Docker Compose
docker-compose up --build

The Dockerfile shall use a python:3.11-slim base image, copy the backend directory, install Python dependencies, and expose port 8000. The docker-compose.yml shall mount the SQLite database file as a persisted volume and map port 8000 to the host.

11.4 Database Migration Strategy

Schema changes shall be managed through Alembic migrations: 

# Generate a new migration after model changes
alembic revision --autogenerate -m "description_of_change"

# Apply pending migrations
alembic upgrade head

# Rollback one migration
alembic downgrade -1

The prototype shall seed demo data automatically on first startup. The seed data shall include at least one demo patient (MHT-000001), sample appointments, bills, lab results, allergies, and chronic conditions to enable immediate evaluation without manual data entry.

11.5 Production Deployment Recommendations

If the prototype proceeds to production, the following migrations are recommended:

  1. Database: Switch from SQLite to PostgreSQL by setting OFFLINE_MODE=False and configuring POSTGRES_URL in the .env file.
  2. TLS Termination: Deploy behind Nginx or Caddy as a reverse proxy to provide HTTPS encryption.
  3. Secret Rotation: Replace all default secret keys (SECRET_KEY, PATIENT_SECRET_KEY) with cryptographically random values generated via openssl rand -hex 32.
  4. CORS Restriction: Replace the development wildcard CORS_ORIGINS=* with the specific production domain.
  5. Caching Layer: Enable Redis for API response caching and WebSocket pub/sub to reduce database load under concurrent access.
  6. Backup Automation: Schedule regular database backups using the provided deploy.ps1 script or cron-based equivalent.
  7. Monitoring: Implement health check endpoints (/health) and structured logging for integration with monitoring tools such as Prometheus and Grafana.

12. Conclusion & Future Work

12.1 Design Summary

This document has presented a comprehensive system design specification for the MUST Health Tech (MHT) prototype — an Electronic Health Record and Hospital Management System proposed for the MUST Teaching Hospital. The design covers the full spectrum of hospital operations through seventeen functional modules organised across two user portals (clinical staff and patient self-service), supported by a RESTful API backend and an offline-capable single-page frontend.

The key design highlights of the proposed system include:

  • A dual-portal architecture with distinct navigation models (sidebar for staff, bottom tabs for patients) and separate authentication domains to ensure appropriate access control for each user population.
  • Offline-first operation achieved through a service worker with dual caching strategies, an IndexedDB local database for structured data caching, and a background synchronisation queue for offline mutations.
  • Progressive Web App deployment enabling installation on user devices without app store distribution, with full-screen display and offline launch capability.
  • A comprehensive clinical data model spanning 35 database tables covering patient demographics, visits, vital signs, clinical documentation, laboratory, pharmacy, billing, inventory, critical care, dialysis, governance, and notifications.
  • Localised features including mobile money payment integration (Mpamba, Airtel Money, Malawian banks), bilingual patient rights education (English/Chichewa), and anonymous corruption reporting.

12.3 Proposed Development Phases

The prototype development is proposed to proceed in three phases:

  1. Phase 1 — Core Infrastructure (Weeks 1-4): Backend setup with FastAPI and SQLAlchemy, database schema creation, JWT authentication system, patient registration API, frontend SPA shell with login and admin sidebar, patient portal login. Deliverable: working login for both portals with patient registration.
  2. Phase 2 — Clinical Modules (Weeks 5-10): Appointments, clinical notes, vital signs, laboratory, pharmacy, billing, and patient portal read features (appointments, bills, results). Deliverable: end-to-end clinical workflow from registration through discharge and billing.
  3. Phase 3 — Advanced Features (Weeks 11-14): Dashboard and analytics, inpatient/critical care/dialysis modules, governance reporting, offline/PWA support, notifications, patient portal More panel, responsive design polish, and documentation. Deliverable: complete prototype ready for evaluation.

12.4 Future Development Opportunities

Beyond the prototype phase, the following enhancements are identified for future development:

  1. Health Information Exchange: Implementation of HL7 FHIR R4 APIs for interoperability with national health systems (DHIS2, Malawi EMRS, electronic immunisation registry).
  2. Clinical Decision Support: Drug-drug interaction checking, allergy alerting, and evidence-based treatment protocol suggestions integrated into the prescribing workflow.
  3. Advanced Analytics and AI: Machine learning models for early warning of patient deterioration, disease outbreak prediction from surveillance data, and automated radiology report triage.
  4. Telemedicine Integration: Video consultation capabilities with real-time vital signs streaming for remote specialist review, particularly relevant for MUST's role as a referral centre.
  5. Biometric Patient Identification: Fingerprint or iris scanning to eliminate duplicate patient records and ensure accurate identification, especially for patients without formal identification documents.
  6. Laboratory Instrument Interface: Direct HL7/LIS integration with laboratory analysers for automated result capture, reducing manual transcription errors.
  7. Mobile Money API Integration: Direct server-to-server API connection with Airtel Malawi and TNM for automated payment processing and reconciliation.
  8. Multi-Facility Support: Extension to support district hospital networks with central data aggregation, referral tracking, and cross-facility record access.

12.5 Conclusion

The MUST Health Tech (MHT) system design presented in this document offers a comprehensive, context-appropriate solution for the digital transformation of the MUST Teaching Hospital. The proposed architecture balances the need for clinical functionality with the realities of operating in a resource-constrained environment, prioritising offline resilience, local language support, and accessible technology choices. The prototype, once developed according to this specification, will provide a demonstrable proof-of-concept that can inform future investment in health information systems at MUST and serve as a reference model for similar initiatives across Malawi's healthcare sector. The successful implementation of this system has the potential to improve patient care quality, operational efficiency, and data-driven decision-making at one of Malawi's premier teaching hospitals.
